The core thesis

The winning enterprise AI architecture will not be either “stable suite” or “autonomous swarm.” It will be a resilient, modular operating fabric.

Stable systems of record, sovereign data, API-accessible workflows, governed agents, model choice, human accountability and outcome-based economics. Enterprises that separate resilience from rigidity — and modularity from chaos — will compound advantage as frontier models converge and agent capabilities diffuse.

Inside the report

Ten chapters, one doctrine.

  1. The winning enterprise AI architecture will be neither “stable suite” nor “autonomous swarm,” but a resilient modular operating fabric: stable systems of record, sovereign data, governed agents, model choice, human accountability and outcome-based economics.

  2. Adoption is broad but scaled value remains uneven — and capital intensity, compute, power and sovereignty have become board-level inputs to strategy, not IT line items.

  3. Separate what must be resilient (data, identity, audit, compliance) from what must be modular (models, agents, workflows). Enterprises that split resilience from rigidity — and modularity from chaos — compound advantage.

  4. How the stack evolves from monolithic SaaS suites to an agentic operating fabric: API-accessible workflows and governed agent layers operating over stable systems of record.

  5. Where agent-native startups actually win against incumbents: workflow depth, outcome-based pricing and sovereignty-friendly deployment — not feature parity with the suites.

  6. The investable gaps this thesis opens: agent governance and evaluation infrastructure, vertical operating fabrics, and data-sovereignty tooling.

  7. Outcome-based pricing, sovereignty premiums and human-AI symbiosis reshape vendor economics and the roles people play inside intelligent organizations.

  8. The forces shaping the rest of the decade: frontier-model convergence, $285.9B in US private AI investment, a 29.6 GW data-center power build-out and national AI capacity as geopolitics.

  9. Agents are useful yet unreliable enough to require controls: excessive agency, privilege misuse, prompt injection, nondeterministic outputs and cost spikes demand layered governance and auditability.

  10. A sequenced adoption roadmap — govern, modularize, instrument outcomes — plus the appendices' agentic SaaS diligence checklists for buyers and builders.
